The UK government just gave small businesses free AI productivity tools.

The Department for Science, Innovation and Technology has launched the SKAI programme and the PRIMES framework — structured, free tools to help UK employers assess their AI readiness, upskill their workforce, and measure actual productivity gains. With 54% of UK businesses using AI but fewer than one in four seeing it embedded in core processes, this is the government's first formal attempt to close the adoption gap.

What launched: DSIT has published the SKAI (Skills and Knowledge for AI) programme alongside the PRIMES framework — two interconnected tools for UK employers. SKAI provides workforce upskilling guidance structured around AI roles and AI-adjacent roles. PRIMES gives employers a self-assessment framework to measure their current AI maturity and identify where productivity gains are being left on the table.

Why the government is doing this now

The data points are stark. British Chambers of Commerce reported in March 2026 that 54% of UK businesses are now using AI. AWS data from June shows only 24% have AI embedded in core business processes. The resulting 30-point gap — between using AI occasionally and deploying it consistently — represents what researchers are calling the fumble period: the time between buying a tool and learning to use it productively.

Independent research suggests UK businesses are leaving significant economic value unrealised. The IMF has forecast productivity gains from AI of between 0.9 and 1.5 percent per year for the UK. Workers with AI-adjacent skills already command a 23% wage premium. The gap between companies that deploy AI consistently and those that experiment and abandon is not a technology gap — it is a skills and process gap.

What SKAI actually provides

SKAI is a competency framework — it maps what AI-related skills different roles in a business need and provides structured guidance on how to develop them. For a small service business, this means:

  • A skills map showing which AI capabilities are most relevant to your role types (admin, operations, customer-facing, management)
  • Structured learning paths aligned to free and low-cost training resources
  • A vocabulary for discussing AI capability development with employees without needing an IT background

What PRIMES actually provides

PRIMES is a self-assessment tool — a structured questionnaire that positions your business on an AI maturity scale and identifies specific gaps. For small businesses, the most useful output is the gap analysis: it tells you not just where you are but what the next step is, expressed in practical terms rather than technical jargon.

Previously, this kind of structured assessment was only accessible through paid consultancy or enterprise-grade AI readiness programmes costing thousands of pounds. PRIMES makes a version of it free and accessible to sole traders and micro-businesses as well as larger SMEs.

Important caveat

Government-launched tools often lag the cutting edge of what commercial AI tools can do. SKAI and PRIMES are most useful as a structured baseline and vocabulary, not as a guide to which specific AI products to use. They are excellent starting points for businesses that have not yet formalised their AI approach — less useful for businesses already running consistent AI workflows.
