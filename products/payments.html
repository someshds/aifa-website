The Problem

The job that got done but didn’t get paid for 6 weeks

Service work has a chronic payment lag problem. You quote. You do the work. You send an invoice from your accounting software. The customer says they’ll “sort it tomorrow.” Three follow-up emails later, the money lands — or it doesn’t, and you have an awkward conversation that puts the relationship under stress.

Late payment is the #1 cause of small-business cashflow stress in the UK. Average invoice gets paid 14 days late, and 1 in 5 invoices is paid more than 30 days late. That’s not customers being malicious — it’s the friction of "now I need to find my card / log into the bank app / remember to do it."

The fix isn’t harassing customers. The fix is making it easier to pay than to procrastinate. Take the deposit at the point of booking. Send a one-tap pay link the moment work is complete. Set up the recurring service on direct debit from day one. Friction down, cashflow up.
