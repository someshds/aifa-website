Common Questions

Things people ask before signing up

What exactly is an "AI agent"?

An AI agent is a piece of software trained to do a specific job in your business autonomously — answer the phone, reply to leads, book appointments, respond to reviews. Unlike a chatbot that just answers questions, an agent takes actions: it books the slot, sends the link, updates the CRM, and hands off to a human when needed.

Do I have to use all the agents at once?

No. Most clients start with the single agent that addresses their biggest leak — usually the Voice Agent (for missed calls) or the Sales Agent (for slow lead response). You can add more agents over time, and they're built to work together as a connected team when you do.

Will the agent sound robotic or off-brand?

No. Each agent is trained on your tone of voice, your services, and your specific phrasing. We do a review with you before launch and adjust until it sounds like your business. Most customers can't tell they're dealing with AI — and the ones who can tend to be impressed by the speed.

What happens when something needs a human?

Every agent is built with hand-off logic. When it hits a high-value situation, an edge case, or a request it shouldn't handle alone, it transfers to your team — by call transfer, SMS, or app notification — with the full context attached.

How long does it take to go live?

Most single agents are live within 5–7 working days from the discovery call. A full multi-agent team takes a little longer depending on the number of integrations and how much existing content we have to train on.

What does it cost?

Pricing depends on which agents you deploy and how many conversations you expect per month. See our pricing page for current tiers, or book a demo and we'll quote your exact setup.
