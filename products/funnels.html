The Problem

The traffic that arrived and left without a trace

You drove traffic. Maybe an ad spend, maybe a Facebook post that did well, maybe a podcast mention. People clicked. Then they hit your homepage and... left.

Homepages are bad at converting traffic. They’re built for general orientation, not specific intent. A homepage asks the visitor to figure out what to do next; a funnel takes that decision away. Dedicated landing pages convert 5–15× better than general homepages for the same offer — not because they’re prettier, but because they have one job and they do that job.

The hidden cost of not having proper funnels is paying for traffic that doesn’t convert. Every paid click that lands on a homepage instead of a focused opt-in page is mostly wasted spend. Every podcast mention that sends listeners to your domain root instead of a tailored landing page is brand awareness, not lead generation.

Funnels are how you take traffic seriously.
