Common Questions

Things people ask before signing up

Which review platforms can you monitor?

Google Business Profile, Facebook, Trustpilot, TripAdvisor, Yelp, Booking.com, and industry-specific sites like Checkatrade or Bark. We can also bring in any review platform with an API. All surfaced in one unified review dashboard.

Won't customers find review-prompting annoying?

Not when done right. We use SMS rather than email (95%+ open rate), keep the request short ("How was your visit today?"), and prompt at the right moment — usually 24–72 hours after the experience. Most customers happy to oblige; respect-the-no behaviour built in.

Is routing negative reviews to a private form against the rules?

We never block anyone from leaving a public review — that's against Google's terms. We simply route low-rating responses to also leave private feedback first, which lets you respond and resolve before they decide whether to post publicly. Most platforms encourage this approach.

Do you actually respond to reviews for me?

By default, AI drafts the responses (matching your brand voice from training) and you approve them with one tap. Want fully managed? We have a managed-response add-on where our team writes and posts every reply within 24 hours. Most clients start with the AI-draft approach and use it forever.

How does this help my local SEO?

Google's local algorithm rewards review recency, review velocity (steady stream of new reviews), and review responses. A reputation engine that consistently generates new reviews and ensures every one gets a thoughtful response is one of the highest-leverage local-SEO moves you can make.

What about review gating compliance?

Our system follows current Google guidance. The pre-routing rating question is acceptable under Google's terms because every customer can still leave a public review at any time — we just make sure unhappy ones also get private follow-up first. Fully transparent, fully compliant.

The Problem

The 4.5-star business that looks invisible

You do great work. Your customers love you. But on Google, you have 11 reviews. Your competitor down the road has 187. When a prospect compares the two, they don’t care that yours are higher quality on average — they care that the other one looks more proven.

Most service businesses dramatically under-collect reviews from happy customers. Less than 10% of satisfied customers leave a review unprompted — not because they don’t want to, but because the friction of opening Google, finding your listing, clicking the right button, and writing something coherent is just enough work that they don’t bother.

Meanwhile, the unhappy ones leave reviews instantly. That asymmetry creates a public-image problem that distorts the picture buyers see. A real reputation management system fixes both sides: make it easy for happy customers to review, and respond fast and well to negative ones.

What We Build

A reputation engine that runs in the background

We build an automated review request flow that triggers a few days after every job, appointment, or purchase. Customer gets an SMS: "How did we do today? Reply 1–5." If they reply 4 or 5, they get a one-tap link straight to your Google review page (or Facebook, or Trustpilot, depending on what you’re building). If they reply 1–3, they get routed to a private feedback form so you can fix the issue before it becomes a public review.

On top of that, we monitor every review platform you care about and surface every new review in one dashboard. AI-drafted responses for every review — you read, tweak if needed, send. No 5-star review goes unacknowledged, no 1-star review goes unanswered for days.

Result: review volume goes up by 3–5× in 90 days for most clients, average star rating ticks up because you’re catching unhappy customers before they post publicly, and your local search ranking improves as Google rewards businesses with active, recent review activity.
