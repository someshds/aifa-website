The Problem

The 14 follow-ups that didn't happen this week

Every service business has a long tail of work that should happen but doesn’t. The thank-you email after a job. The review request three days later. The check-in a month after that. The seasonal reminder. The win-back text for the client who hasn’t booked in 6 months. The follow-up to the quote that went out 10 days ago.

None of it is hard. All of it is high-value. And almost none of it gets done consistently — because doing it manually means someone has to remember, and remembering is the most fragile part of any business.

The result is leaked revenue. Industry studies put the value of consistent follow-up at 25–40% of total revenue for service businesses, and yet most teams admit they manage maybe a third of what they should. That gap — between what should happen and what actually happens — is the gap workflow automations close.

What We Build

Workflows that do the boring, repeatable work for you

We build visual, drag-and-drop workflows that react to anything your customers do (or don't do). Form filled? Trigger a welcome sequence. Job marked complete? Schedule a review request three days later. Quote not accepted in 7 days? Send a soft nudge. Contact gone quiet for 60 days? Drop them into a re-engagement campaign.

Each workflow can fire any combination of emails, SMS, WhatsApp messages, internal team notifications, calendar bookings, tags, pipeline movements, task assignments, payment requests, or external API calls. Branch logic lets the workflow take different paths depending on what the contact does — replied, clicked, booked, ignored.

Crucially, you don’t need to be a developer. We build the workflow with you in plain English, then you can clone it, tweak it, and stack new variations whenever a new pattern emerges in your business.

Common Questions

Things people ask before signing up

Do I need technical skills to use this?

No. Workflows are built visually — you drag triggers, conditions, and actions onto a canvas and connect them. We build the first set with you, walk you through it live, and you can clone and tweak from there.

What can a workflow actually trigger on?

Almost anything: form submission, calendar booking, payment, missed call, opportunity stage change, tag added/removed, email opened/clicked, SMS reply received, appointment no-show, date-based events (e.g. anniversaries), inactivity timeouts, and webhook events from external apps.

Can workflows talk to my existing tools?

Yes. Native integrations with the most common stacks (Stripe, QuickBooks, Xero, Google Workspace, Mailchimp, Zapier, n8n, Make), plus webhook in/out for anything bespoke. If it has an API, we can talk to it.

How do you stop them spamming customers?

Multiple safeguards: quiet hours so messages don't fire at 2am, frequency caps so contacts can't be hit by overlapping workflows, unsubscribe handling, DND respect, and full audit trails per contact so you can see every message they've had from you.

Can a workflow update my CRM pipeline automatically?

Yes — that's one of the most useful things they do. Quote sent → move to "Quote Out" stage. Booking confirmed → move to "Booked". Payment received → move to "Won". The pipeline reflects reality without anyone manually dragging cards.

What if I want to A/B test sequences?

Built in. Split-test subject lines, send times, message variants, or whole sequence branches. The platform reports on which variant converts better, and you can let the winner take over automatically.
