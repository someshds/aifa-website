The Problem

The booking that took six emails to confirm

Someone wants to book you. They suggest Wednesday. You suggest Thursday. They come back with Friday morning. You say Friday afternoon. By the fifth email, both of you have lost momentum — and at least 30% of those threads quietly die before anyone commits.

Meanwhile, the ones that do convert end up double-booked because your diary lives in three places. Or the customer no-shows because the reminder never went out. Or the rescheduling request landed in a Spam folder and now nobody’s sure if it’s on or off.

The economic damage of bad booking infrastructure is bigger than most service businesses realise. No-show rates above 10% are common when reminders aren’t automated. 30% of customers will simply give up if booking takes more than three clicks. And every back-and-forth email costs roughly five minutes of your time — multiplied by every booking, every day.

A proper booking system closes all of that — lets the customer pick their own slot from your real availability, sends the right reminders at the right times, and feeds the booking into the rest of your business automatically.

What We Build

A booking calendar that works the way your business actually runs

We configure your calendar around your reality — not a generic Calendly template. Service durations, buffer times between appointments, blackout periods, multi-staff routing, location-specific availability, recurring schedules, group sessions, paid bookings — whatever your business actually needs.

Customers see a clean booking page with your branding, pick a real-time available slot, fill in any qualifying questions you need, and (if relevant) pay a deposit. They get an instant confirmation, automatic reminders by SMS and email, and the option to reschedule themselves without picking up the phone.

On your side, every booking lands in your calendar (Google, Outlook, Apple, whatever) AND in your CRM, with the full booking context — what they booked, what they answered, what they paid, when they last visited. One source of truth.
