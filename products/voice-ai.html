The Problem

The call that rang out at 7:42 pm

It’s Tuesday evening. A potential customer is on their phone, looking up a tradesperson who can come out tomorrow. They find your number. They call.

It rings out. Maybe they leave a voicemail, maybe they don’t. Either way, by the time you check the phone the next morning, they’ve already booked the other guy. Industry studies put missed-call abandonment for UK service businesses at around 62% — meaning more than 6 in 10 first-time callers who don’t get a human will not call back. They click the next result in Google.

Even when callers do leave a voicemail, the speed-to-callback problem is brutal. Respond within 5 minutes and you’re up to 100× more likely to win the job than waiting an hour. Wait until tomorrow and the game is essentially over.

You can’t hire enough humans to cover every hour. You can’t expect your team to answer the phone in the middle of dinner. The AI Voice Receptionist closes that gap — not by replacing your people, but by making sure no first call ever falls into a black hole.

What We Build

A receptionist that knows your business inside out

Our AI Voice Receptionist isn’t a generic call menu. It’s a fully-trained voice agent that sounds like a real person, knows your services, prices, coverage area, opening hours, and the specific qualifying questions that matter for your industry — and it answers calls in a natural, conversational way.

It plugs straight into your existing business number (or we give you a new one), and from the moment it picks up, it can greet by name, capture caller details, answer common questions, check live calendar availability, book the appointment, and either send the caller a confirmation by SMS or transfer the call to your mobile if it’s something only a human can handle.

Every call is recorded, transcribed, and logged to the contact’s timeline in your CRM — so your team picks up the next morning with the full context already there.

Common Questions

Things people ask before signing up

Will the AI sound robotic?

No. We use natural-voice models tuned to your accent and tone preferences (e.g. British, US, neutral). Most callers do not realise they’re speaking to AI in the first 30 seconds, and the ones that do tend to be impressed rather than off-put. We do a voice review with you before launch and adjust until you’re happy.

What happens when a call needs a real person?

The AI is trained to detect “hand-off” situations — high-value calls, complaints, edge cases, or requests it doesn’t have an answer for. When it detects one, it transfers the call to your mobile (or a designated number) seamlessly, with a brief whisper telling you who’s on the line and why.

If you’re unavailable, it captures the details, books a callback slot, and sends a confirmation SMS to the caller.

Can it use my existing business number?

Yes. We can either port your existing number into the system, or set it up so calls to your existing number forward through the AI receptionist transparently. Most clients keep the same number their customers already know.

How long does setup take?

Typically 5–7 working days from the discovery call. Number porting (if needed) can add a few days. We always do a full test-call rehearsal with you before flipping it live to real customers.

Is call recording GDPR-compliant?

Yes. The opening greeting includes a short notification that calls are recorded for quality and training purposes — which is the standard UK GDPR-compliant approach. All recordings and transcripts are stored on UK/EU data centres with proper retention controls. Full details in our privacy policy.

What about Sunday calls or bank holidays?

That’s where this product earns its keep. The AI runs 24/7/365 with no overtime, no sick days, and no Bank-Holiday Monday surcharge. Some clients report that 30–40% of bookings now come from hours when no human would have answered the phone.
